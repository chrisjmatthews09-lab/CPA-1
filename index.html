The "Colorado Wedge" Thesis

The Play: Acquire a foundational CPA firm (541211) in the Denver/Boulder/Fort Collins corridor with a stable compliance base ($1M+ revenue). Use this trust anchor to cross-sell high-margin CAS (Client Advisory Services) and payroll, while modernizing the tech stack.

Why Colorado? The Front Range has a high density of growing SMBs (tech, construction, services) facing complex multi-state tax issues due to remote work. Supply of CPAs is constrained (aging demographic), creating pricing power for firms that can solve staffing through near-shoring or automation.

The Deal Killer: Avoid firms with >40% revenue concentration in low-margin 1040 individual tax returns (unless HNW) or owners working 2,500+ hours/year without a succession team.

Roll-up & Wedge Strategy

The "Platform + Tuck-in" playbook.

The "Hub & Spoke" Inorganic Growth Model

1. The Platform (The Wedge)

Acquire a $1.2M - $1.5M SDE CPA firm with strong local reputation, decent (but not perfect) tech, and a partner looking to retire in 2-3 years. This provides cash flow and the "CPA" brand halo.

2. The Tuck-ins

Acquire smaller ($200k - $400k SDE) tax or bookkeeping practices. Migrate their clients to your centralized tech stack and pricing model. Eliminate their overhead (rent, admin).
